How do I Implement Keyword-Driven On-Page SEO

Following the keyword research phase, I executed a comprehensive on-page SEO strategy for my Chinese tattoo design shop. The goal was to move beyond simple keyword placement and build a site architecture optimized for both human users and AI-driven search engines.

Step 1: Metadata & HTML Tag Optimization

Based on the keyword mapping framework outlined in my blog "How do I Conduct Keyword Research for E-commerce", I implemented the following on-page SEO optimizations:

Title Tags: Front-Loading Primary Keywords

For every page, I placed the primary keyword at the beginning of the title tag and within the first 100 words of on-page content.

Homepage example:

Blog post example:

Meta Descriptions: Benefit-Driven Messaging

With AI assistance, I drafted descriptions to include clear CTAs such as "Download your stencil today", "Get tattoo-ready files for your next ink", and "Instant download", helping drive higher organic engagement.

Product page example:

Header hierarchy: Clear and Structured

I created a strict and logical header structure to reinforce topical relevance and readability, ensuring each page targets a single keyword cluster.

For example, for a product page:

  • H1: Primary Keyword (e.g., Karma | Handwritten Chinese Calligraphy Design)
  • H2: Strategic Sub-topics (e.g., Product Details, Why Choose This Design)
  • H3: Specific Attributes (e.g., Script Style, License Information)

Images: Descriptive Alt Text

Beyond aesthetics, I optimized images for "Image Search" by using descriptive filenames and rich Alt Text.

For example:

Schema Markup: Improved Visibility

I implemented JSON-LD structured data to improve visibility and rich result eligibility:

  • Product Schema: For tattoo designs (price, availability)
  • Article Schema: For blog posts (headline, publish date)

Open Graph Tags: Link Previews Generation

I also added Open Graph Tags to boost social engagement.

Step 2: Linking Structure & Content Strategy

My linking strategy was designed to distribute link equity across the site and keep users engaged in the conversion funnel.

Anchor Text: Clean and Descriptive

I replaced generic "click here" links with descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text to provide better context to crawlers.

Descriptive anchor text

URL Strings: Clear and Explanatory

I also used readable, keyword-aligned URLs for both products and blog content.

Clean URL strings

Internal linking: The "Hub and Spoke" Model

Every blog post includes internal links to relevant product pages, often featuring mock-up images for visualizations

Blog internal image links

Product pages include a "More Similar Designs" section with clear titles and visual previews

This structure encourages longer on-site sessions and signals strong user engagement to search engines.

Outbound Links: Authority Signals

To build trust, I linked specific linguistic explanations to academic and museum sources, reinforcing the cultural accuracy of my designs.

Topic Cluster Strategy

In blog section, I established a pillar post "How to Choose Your Chinese Tattoo: Expert Tips From a Calligrapher" as the central hub, which contains four sections, including meanings, styles, placement, and common mistakes. And Each section is expanded into a standalone supporting blog post, linking back to this pillar post to build topical authority.

Topic cluster blog posts

General Content & GEO Principles

In the era of Generative Engine Optimization (GEO), I tried to ensure the content was structured for AI agents (like Gemini and GPT-4) to easily parse and cite.

  • Semantic Keyword Integration: I integrated more "LSI" (Latent Semantic Indexing) keywords such as ink, calligraphy, stencil, brush strokes, and symbolism to create a natural, high-quality reading experience that avoids keyword stuffing.

LSI keywords example

  • SGE Optimization: I structured product and blog content into short paragraphs and bulleted lists to improve readability and make it easier for AI models to extract answers.

Bullet points example

  • Multimedia integration: To increase engagement and dwell time, I embedded handwriting process videos (also builds trust and showcases expertise).

Multimedia example

E-E-A-T: Trust Signals

To meet Google's Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness standards:

  • Author Bylines & Bios: Every article links to an "About the Author" page, establishing credibility as a Chinese calligrapher rather than a generic content creator

Summary

Based on the result of keyword research, I implemented on-page SEO by optimizing metadata, html tags, linking structure, as well as GEO to boost user engagement and improve organic traffic performance.
