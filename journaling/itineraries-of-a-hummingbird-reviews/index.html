When I talk about suicide, I invariably receive emotional objections and attempts to dissuade me, so I stopped discussing the topic with anyone. However, for me, it's a very easy and safe topic. Suicidal fantasies are indeed a part of my daily life; I think about them every day. It's precisely because I'm certain I will never actively choose to end my own life that I allow myself to indulge in these suicidal fantasies. Having ruled out the possibility of carrying it out at this moment (which is Cioran's view that "one should always postpone suicide"), thinking about it brings only an immense sense of security. This is currently the greatest source of security in my life. My life, my body, is the only thing I am certain I can completely control.

For me now, this is probably the foundation for maintaining a normal life.

To maximize my control over my life, I will try my best to keep my body healthy. To keep my mind clear, I will learn more and read more books. The premise of this positive life is that I can choose to end it at any time. In other words, it is the thought of suicide that makes me positive; its existence constantly reminds me that my life is entirely in my own hands.

Although I'm extremely reluctant to admit it, it was indeed depression that forced me to constantly contemplate the concept of death for a period of time. But if I were to acknowledge that depression helped me grow, it would seem as if the pain I suffered because of it all gained meaning. This is hard to accept.
